My Netcraft

Course management System for Netcraft Academy

As a stiudent of Netcraft Academy, I decided to build a new system the Academy and its growing volumen of work for my final project, I did the UX Designing and worked with the Academy management team.


The target audience was the students and the management team.They all faced a heavy workload. While mainly working manually, in order to give enough attention to their students it was inevitable to automate most processes and be more efficent.


From the teachers point of view. The challenge was to get the most relevant information for each day without missing the essential tasks they had to do. As for the students. I wished to organize the course info to help them on course. Also, allow teachers to track their progress to back them up.

Research and Findings



At first, I conducted twently students questionnaires and then interviews.


I chose those research methods because I needed to answer my research questions-Are the students using the Academy tools? What are the teachers pain points?


After that, I interviewed fve faculty memebers to understand their needs and dally routine in the Academy. I was curious about their prioritizing system since, as a a stundent. I sometimes felt my chances to speak with my professor hanged on the shoulders of luck.


The main insight I got was that the large workload was deeply affected by using many different platforms like google drive, Gmail, Facebook, Facebook Messenger and Whatsapp-just to comunicate with the students.



Main Findings


  • 80% of the students thought the course materials were
    inaccessible of which 10% claimed that there were
    materials they did not find at all.

  • 40% of the respondents did not use the course schedule
    at all, of which about half claimed that they did not
    know that they had access to it at all.

  • 20% of students did not understand on which platform
    they should contact the lecturer.

  • One main lecturer in charge of dozens of students.

  • Manual work for the most part inability to keep track of
    all the many changes that happen regularly.

  • Fighting fires as a method of work.

Competitor Audit


Since "My Netcraft" is an app designed for one unique user, and no such app exists today, it was not possible to conduct competitor research. What could have been done was to see how others had gone it and to take from them important features that had worked well in previous cases I used for that purpous 3 different universities apps and websites and 3 companies that developed different teaching system.



Define & Ideate


Personas




Based on the interviews, I set up two personas. I referred to them throughout the entire product development process. I used User research to develop the persona. Since it is is based on a specific Academy. I was able to establish my persona in a very accurate way on a real person, with slight changes- to keep his privacy.


Working with the persona in mind made it much easier to focus on the correct Hierarchy of information.

User Journey


I mapped aout the users steps to see how I could simplify their journey to help them reach their most important goals with the product. I did it by asking them what they would like to improve in their experience and what things consume most of their time.


Given the sheer number of task each user faces which is time-consuming when inefficient, the information hierarchy was paramount.




User Flow


I created two user scenarios as it is a complex system and has countiess possible scenarios that happen daily. Both of them describe a very common situation that happens to the most recent users.




Wireframing


Low Fidelity Wireframes


When I start the wireframes stage, I have a lot of information that needs to be “Translated” to the way we see it on the screen it may sound simple, but it can be pretty challenging.


The two magic words are Staging and Iteration.


Staging- In UX Design, we can’t skip any stage. Each stage helps to build the stage after. The more steps, the better.


Iteration- Each object will transit at least 3-4 times throughout the process. This multiple transitioning is what makes the outcome better.


In the end, I had three different versions. The difference between them mainly was the use of the menus. The final version allows the user to organize his home page thanks to flexible information boxes.

What have I learned from this project?


Since it was my final project, sadly, I didn’t use it on real users in the end I have 100% believe in it, and I genuinely think it could save at least 50% of the time for the Academy staff.


The biggest problem was around the number of day-to-day tasks and their prioritization. Many of the things were under the deadline and needed to be placed in such a way that would allow immediate handing in parallel with the other more critical and routine task.


I overcame these issues by repeatedly iterating the dashboard functions. At the end of a long and cumbersome process, it amounted to 2-3 clicks.


In addition, there was a factor of apprehension as I was trying to characterize an app for an institution that teaches me to characterize apps. A little pretentious on my part. Like making shoes for a shoemarkers.


I solved this problem by trying to do the best I could.
